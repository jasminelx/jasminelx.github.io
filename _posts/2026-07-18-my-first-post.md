This is a blog post. You're writing in plain text — Jekyll turns it into a
properly formatted page automatically. The bit between the `---` lines at the
top is the post's settings: its title and the teaser shown on the blog page.

A blank line starts a new paragraph. That's most of what you need to know.

## A subheading looks like this

Start a line with `##` and a space. Smaller subheadings use `###`.

You can make text **bold** with two asterisks, *italic* with one, and
[a link looks like this](https://example.com).
