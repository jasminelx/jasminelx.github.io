Hello! If you're reading this, you've had to click on several things, so thank you for sticking with me to this point and clicking on them. 

My name is Jasmine, I'm 21 and I recently graduated from King's College London. There, I studied Artificial Intelligence. But I also:

- Ran improv sessions for the Kings Players theatre society
- Directed Ionesco's 'Rhinoceros' for that same theatre society
- Wrote two short plays for, you guessed it, the Kings Players!

My university career most definitely consisted of a pull between two of my biggest passions: ensuring the future of AI goes well, and theatre! These two pursuits are far more connected than they initially seem; I want a future where all humans can enjoy creativity and the arts; I want to use my creative skills to ensure that future! 

Alongside all of that, I'm a professional harpist! This started two summers ago, when I started busking in the city centre of Newcastle. From that, people in the streets heard my music and I booked several weddings! My favourite styles to perform currently are celtic pieces (Kim Robertson is one of my favourite arrangers), Bollywood romantic ballads and Paraguayan traditional harp music. 

So, there's some background about me. Onto the future!

## What now?

Here are my thoughts:

- I am passionate about working in the field of AI, specifically in fieldbuilding
- I want to use my strong communicative abilities I have grown through theatre
- I want more people to feel like they have a say in the future of AI

So far, these thoughts have led me to LIaiSE, a nonprofit organisation committed to helping generalist professionals find their place within the AI safety world. Within LIaiSE, I'm responsible for content and communications, as well as helping with in-person events and general team stuff! 

Working with LIaiSE has been my first concrete step into the field, and I'm really proud of what we're doing there! So far, I've had the opportunity to meet so many interesting and inspiring people already. 

I also recently completed BlueDot Impact's AGI strategy course, which similarly really made me aware of the work being done on AI safety, and where I might want to go into. Overall, the last two months have been a time for me to learn, try new things, and understand where I fit into it all. 

With all that in mind, there's still one thing I really need: a job!
